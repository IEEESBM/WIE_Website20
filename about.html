FAMOUS WOMEN PART OF WIE

Ramalatha Marimuthu

Ramalatha Marimuthu is presently working in Kumaraguru College of Technology. She is experienced in motivating and training students on skill development and forming a peer network. She was the chair of WIEC for 2011-2012. Her main focus is on projects and ideas for societal problems and solutions for the betterment of rural areas. She launched the project “Sangamam”, which aimed at transfer of technology to rural areas thereby improving the quality of life in rural areas. She has won several awards like 2009 Mentor Award by the Secretariat for the disabled, Life Time achievement award by Lions Club international, recently she won 2016 WIE Inspiring Member Award.

Liang Downey

Liang Downey works as a Business Executive for IBM’s New Energy and Global Industry. She earned a Master Degree in Electrical Engineering and received her MBA in Finance from Wayne State University. She is an experienced professional in consulting, program management and emerging technology development across multiple industries. She supported a group of volunteers in IEEE humanitarian technology challenge that aimed at facilitating remote villages with electricity. She promotes development of IOT, analytics and cognitive computing services as these serve as better offerings for customers and solve other technical issues. She is an Adcom member of IEEE TEMS society 2016-2020 and Chair for Women In Engineering of Region-4.

Lee Yoot Khuan

Lee Yoot Khuan did her B.Sc. in aeronautical engineering and is presently an Electrical Engineering faculty member at UiTM(full form to be mentioned) . Her leadership has played an important role in enhancing global visibility of IEEE Malaysia. She was one of the founder members of EMBS, co-founder of SIGHT group in Malaysia and has been in pivotal positions in EMBS and WIE Malaysia section. She has put in efforts for several causes like promoting inclusivity and diversity in engineering and other spheres, promoting equality, STEM education and careers in Malaysia. Her research experience particularly includes analysis and remote display of biomedical signals, neurofeedback of dyslexia children and expertise include neural networks, algorithms, EEG signal processing which are a few to mention. She was awarded 2020 WIE inspiring member for her contribution in the field of engineering and efforts in empowering the future generation.

Paige Kassalen

Paige Kassalen was the only female engineer in the Solar impulse project in which a solar powered airplane was built. She was in the team as an electrical engineer with a background in public relations. She was given a spot in Forbes 30 Under 30 list for her achievements. She graduated from Virginia Tech in 2015 and started working for Covestro, where she got the opportunity of being a part of the Solar Impulse project and grabbed it. This opportunity made her realize people themselves set the boundary between possible and impossible and she decided to dream bigger and try to push the boundaries. She has also served as the chair of WIE Pittsburg chapter during her college days.

Karen Panetta

Karen Panetta is the Dean of Graduate Education for the school of Engineering. She received B.S in Computer engineering and MS and PhD in electrical engineering. She is IEEE-USA president and WIE Magazine Editor-in-chief. She is an active member of IEEE known for her endeavors in teaching, mentoring, creative curriculum development and outreach activities. She was awarded with the nation’s highest award for Engineering, Science and Mathematics Education and Mentoring by Obama in 2011. Dr. Panetta is the recipient of a number of awards of NASA and National Science Foundation research grants, Women of vision award, the 2013 New England Engineers week Leadership Award and many more for social impact, mentoring, and ethics. She is the founder of the International Nerd Girls Program. She is a role model for a number of female young engineers like Page Kassalen. She is the STEM Advisor to the President of Malawi.
